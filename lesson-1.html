Lesson 1: What Is a Guitar?

Overview

This is your first guide! Awesome! To get you situated, we usually have an overview that explains a quick summary of the topic we want to go over with you followed by some of the objectives we want you to have as you learn. Learning with a goal in mind is a powerful thing! At the bottom of the page you'll find links to the separate concept pages to help you reach the objectives and accomplish your goals. We recommend that you go through them in order, but if there is something in specific you're looking for go ahead at whatever order you wish. All we ask is that you feel comfortable with all the objectives before you move on to the next lesson.

So what is a guitar anyway? Some may say a wooden box with strings. Others would say a vehicle to another world. Which is it? We don't think that there's a one size fits all answer to that question, but we do believe in understanding principles. We want you to understand these princples before we even begin plucking strings and strumming chords.


Objectives

The princples that we want to explore the parts of the guitar, the types of guitars there are, the terms used to describe the sound of the guitar, and, probably most important, how to maintain your instrument. To make less vague, we'll brake it down into goals, or objectives, below:

-Be able to identify major parts of a guitar
-Know the differnces between popular types of guitars
-Understand how each type of popular guitar creates it's sound
-Be comfortable with musical terms to describe sound
-Know how to take care of and protect your instrument
