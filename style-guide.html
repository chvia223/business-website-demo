Elevator Pitch

Have you ever watched someone playing the guitar and thought to yourself, “Wow, I wish I could do something cool like that.”? What if I told you you could? I’ve been playing and practicing guitar for over 10 years now and have started to feel like it’s time to share what I’ve learned. I can help you learn the basics and give you enough structure in your practice that you’ll propel your learning fast. You might be able to learn everything on your own, but with me, you won’t have to waste your time searching for the next step. Check out my website to learn more.

Purpose Statement

I’m Christian and I love playing the guitar. It’s my passion and it’s something that I want to share. I remember when I first started learning. I didn’t have the money to pay for formal lessons and I had no clue what I needed to learn to get better or where to find it. My website would be a place for me to share what I have learned in an organized way so newcomers and beginners to guitar can spend less time wandering and more time practicing. I would provide material and techniques that will help people become better guitar players.

Audience Statement

I wish to help all those who are interested in learning the guitar, but have never felt like they had the resources or the know how to learn on their own. I want to keep everything free in order to help as many people as possible. You never know who could become the next Eric Clapton or Joe Bonamassa after all. I expect many of the visitors to the website to be a younger audience between the ages of 13-18. With that in mind, I expect the majority of the devices accessing the site to be laptops or smart phones. Creating an accessible mobile user experience will be necessary.

Misson Statement

I, Christian Viazzo, believe that each person with the desire to learn the guitar should have the experience of doing so with confidence and consistency. That is why I am providing a streamlined guide of the things that have helped me improve over the years. The goal in doing this is to make powerful and productive learning material that is as accessible to as many people as possible. The principles that I will teach, pulled from current popular music, will provide confidence and relevance to those who want to show off their new skills. Expect to stretch and grow as you explore the guitar.

Persona and Scenario Ideas

Name: Luke
Age: 16
Motivation: To learn how to write better music.
Goal: To get a better understanding of how music is structured and how to use that in my guitar practicing.
Technological Proficiency: Capable

Background: As a guitar player, I want to be able to understand how the music I play works because I feel like it will help me write my own music better.

Scenario: Luke gets on the website because he doesn’t quite understand the concept of music being used in a song he’s learning, but he knows the term for the concept being used. Our website should have a tab for common concepts covered by the guides and tutorials. When Luke clicks on the tab it opens to a list. He sees the term he’s looking for and it opens up a list of guides and tutorials where that concept is covered. At this point Luke has options to choose from. From these options he’ll be able to see how the same concept is used in different scenarios.


Name: Michela
Age: 19
Motivation: To learn how to play the guitar and write songs better.
Goal: To be able to learn quickly and clearly and not waste time.
Technological Proficiency: Capable

Background: As a student, I don’t have a lot of time to look around for disconnected parts or guides to learn how to play the guitar, so I would really appreciate a place where I could find everything in one place.

Scenario: Michela gets on the website because she finally has some free time after studying all day. She only has 30 minutes to an hour so she needs to get going quickly. She has been on the website before and wants to start where she left off last time she visited. There should be a tab to continue a guide that was previously opened to get to it quickly otherwise in the list of guides there should be check marks next to guides that have been completed. For technicalities, this can either be tracked by a user account registered with the website or through cookies. Upon clicking on this, Michela should be taken directly to the guide she was working on before as fast as she can. In case she needs a refresher on previously learned material, there should be a list in the margin to show previously completed and currently working on guides that Michela would be able to easily click on and access. It may be good to include a save or bookmark function somewhere obvious to better categorize where Michela leaves off when she needs to leave.
